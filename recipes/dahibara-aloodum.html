Dahibara Aloodum

Description:

Dahibara Aloodum is a type of chaat (snack) originating in Cuttack, Odisha.

The dish is prepared by soaking baras (also called, vadas) in spiced buttermilk (ghola dahi) which is tempered with mustard seeds and curry leaves. Then adding aloodum (hot and spicy potato curry) and ghuguni (pea curry) (optional) to it.

This is a much celebrated dish from Coastal Odisha, and a much loved childhood (and adulthood) treat of mine.

Ingredients:

  • For Bara:

    • Black Gram Lentil/Urad Dal
    • Semolina/Sooji
    • Baking Powder
    • Green chillis
    • Ginger
    • Vegetable Oil
    • Table Salt

  • For Spiced Buttermlik/Ghola Dahi/Dahi Pani:

    • Beaten Curd
    • Table Salt
    • Black Pepper Powder
    • Black Salt
    • Roasted Cumin Powder
    • Red Chilli Powder
    • Chopped Green Chillis
    • Aromatics of Choice (Orange leaves, Mint, Coriander et cetera)

  • For Tempering:

    • Curry Leaves
    • Dried Red Chillis
    • Asafoetida
    • Mustard Seeds

  • For Aloodum:

    • Potatoes
    • Tomatoes
    • Onions
    • Ginger Garlic Paste
    • Cumin Seeds
    • Cumin Powder
    • Coriander Powder
    • Red Chilli Powder
    • Bay Leaves
    • Cloves
    • Cardamom
    • Cinnamon
    • Garam Masala
    • Vegetable Oil
    • Salt

  • For Garnishing:

    • Mint/Podina Chutney
    • Sev
    • Chopped Onions
    • Chopped Coriander
    • Chopped Green Chillis

Steps:

  1. Making Bara:

    1. Soak Urad Dal in Water and let it ferment for six hours
    2. Grind the fermented Urad Dal, Green Chillis, Ginger and Salt into a thick batter
    3. Add Sooji and Baking Powder and beat it with hands to aerate it well
    4. Heat Vegetable Oil in a Karai/Kadai and drop small balls of batter into it once it is hot
    5. Soak the freshly deep-fried Baras in a bowl of Water

  2. Making Spiced Buttermilk/Dahi Pani

    1. In a bowl, beat the Curd to a smooth consistency
    2. Add Salt, Black Salt, Red Chilli Powder, Roasted Cumin Powder, Chopped Green Chillis and mix well
    3. Add water as per desired consistency, taking care not to make it too thin
    4. Add Aromatics of choice

  3. Tempering:

    1. Remove Baras from the soaking bowl and squeeze gently to remove excess water
    2. Add it to the Spiced Buttermilk/Dahi Pani
    3. Heat two table spoons of cooking oil in a tempering pan
    4. Add Mustard Seeds, Curry Leaves, broken Red Chillis and Asafoetida
    5. Pour this tempering on the Dahibara and place it aside for a few hours

  4. Making Aloodum:

    1. In a pressure cooker, boil Potatoes for three whistles, then remove and peel them
    2. Heat some oil to shallow fry, ut potatoes to medium sized pieces and fry them till slight browning here and there
    3. Transfer the shallow fried Potatoes to a kitchen towel to drain the excess oil
    4. In a saucepan heat Oil, add Bay Leaves, Cumin Seeds, Cloves, Cardamom, Cinnamon
    5. Add the chopped Onions and saute until translucent
    6. Add the Ginger Garlic Paste and Green Chillis and saute
    7. Add the chopped Tomatoes, a pinch of Salt and cook until mushy
    8. Add Garam Masala, Cumin Powder, Coriander Powder and Red Chilli Powder and saute them well till you can smell the nice aroma of the sauteed spice
    9. Add the shallow fried Potatoes and Salt per taste
    10. Add some Water to dilute the jhola(gravy) and let it boil for 10 -15 minutes

  5. Assembly:

    1. By this time the Dahibaras must have soaked well and would have turned soft
    2. Press the Dahibara lightly and place is a serving bowl
    3. Add some Aloodum on top of the Dahibara
    4. Sprinkle Black Salt, Red Chilli Powder and Roasted Cumin Powder
    5. Garnish it with Podina Chutney, Sev, chopped Onions, Coriander Leaves and Green Chillis
    6. Serve right away!
